CIVILIZATION

The Ancient

Rajasthani civilization

The historic and ancient civilization of Rajasthan is a rich tapestry of cultures and kingdoms that have flourished over millennia. The region has been home to various civilizations, and has witnessed the rise and fall of several dynasties.

Ancient History

Rajasthan was part of the Indus Valley civilization, associated with the Ahar-Banas and Ochre-Coloured Pottery cultures. The timeline of Rajasthan's history spans from the development of the Sonthi culture (4600 BC) to the emergence of Rajput clans (700 AD).

Medival & Modern Period

The medieval period saw the rise of Rajput clans, such as the Chauhans, Guhilas, and Rathors. The region also witnessed the rule of various dynasties, including the Mauryas, Guptas, and Huns.

Rajputs & Warrior Clans

The 12th century marked a significant period in Rajasthan's history with the emergence of Rajput kingdoms, who played a significant role in shaping the region's history through their valor and chivalry.

Rajasthan is home to color coded cities

One of the fun facts about Rajasthan is that this state is home to colour-coded cities. There is Jaipur: the pink city, Jodhpur: the blue city, Jaisalmer: the golden city and Udaipur: the white city. These nicknames were given due to these cities most dominant colours. You will find plenty of blue houses in Jodhpur, for example, while most of Jaipur buildings are salmon-pink, the desert city of Jaisalmer is golden yellow and Udaipur is full of white buildings.

Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Jaipur, Udaipur

Rajasthan is not only desert

Although Rajasthan is famous for its desert and camel safaris, only 40% of Rajasthan is desert. You will also find hills full of forests, rivers and waterfalls in Rajasthan, which makes for great hiking opportunities. Mount Abu, in the Aravalli Hills, is Rajasthans one and only hill station. It stands at 1,220 m (4,003 ft) above sea level and its a popular destination to escape the heat of this desert state, especially during the summer months.

Mount Abu, Aravalli Hills

The Great Wall of India is in Rajasthan

Did you know that India has a great wall too, just like China?
The wall surrounding Kumbhalgarh Fort is the second-longest continuous wall in the world, right after the famous Great Wall of China. The Indian Wall is 36 km long and was built in the 15th century. This wall protected the fort of the old kingdom of Mewar (present-day Udaipur) from invaders. Its set in the Aravalli Hills, which was the natural border of this kingdom.
